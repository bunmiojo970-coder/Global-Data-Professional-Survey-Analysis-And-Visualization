# Global-Data-Professional-Survey-Analysis-And-Visualization

# Executive Summary:

Using Microsoft Power BI, I transformed and visualized the global data professional survey dataset creating an interactive dashboard using charts and filters to identify trends in salary expectations and career experiences, delivering insights in order to highlight key areas for industry improvement.

# Business Problem:

The aim of this analysis is to identify trends in salary expectations and career experiences, delivering insights in order to highlight key areas for industry improvement.

# Methodology:

1. Data Cleaning and Transformation with Functions and Tools that improved dataset consistency.
2. Charts creating an interactive dashboard which enhanced visualization and communication of key data insights to stakeholders.

# Skills/Tools Used:

Power BI: Remove Columns, Split by delimiter(here, the delimiter was under custom and it was the first part of a bracket or open bracket, Colon was also used as a delimiter to separate another column), Split by Digit to Non-Digit, Replace Values, Add Custom Column with formula
=([#"Q3 - Current Yearly Salary (in USD) - Copy.1"] + [#"Q3 - Current Yearly Salary (in USD) - Copy.2"]) /2),
filters, Duplicate Column, Replace tools.

# Results/Key Insights:

Globally, through the survey, it was mostly very easy to break into Data which was up to 23.49% of survey participants while most data professionals, mostly Data Analysts, prefer Python. Also, the average global age of Data Professionals is approximately 30 years. 
Globally, through the survey, happiness with Work/life balance is at 5.74 while happiness with Salary is at 4.27 out of 10.

The United States had the highest average yearly salary possible which was for Data Scientists at $130,000. The US also had the highest level of happiness with work/life balance and salary which were at 6.38 and 5.00 respectively.

It was mostly very easy for the Canadian Survey participants to break into Data which was at 43.42% which was the highest compared to the other countries. Canada also had a high happiness with work/life balance at 6.06.

India's survey participants revealed a really low happiness with work/life balance and salary at 4.79 and 3.51 respectively.

27% of Survey participants from the UK, which was the highest percentage, found it neither easy nor difficult to break into data.
